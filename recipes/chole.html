CHOLE BHATURE RECIPE

Description:

Chole bhature, a beloved North Indian dish, is a tantalizing combination of spicy chickpea curry (chole) and fluffy fried bread (bhature). This flavorful dish is bursting with aromatic spices, tangy tomatoes, and tender chickpeas, creating a symphony of flavors that is sure to delight your taste buds. Whether enjoyed as a hearty breakfast, satisfying lunch, or festive dinner, chole bhature is a crowd-pleaser that never fails to impress.

Ingredients:

  • For Chole:
    • Chickpeas (Kabuli chana), soaked overnight
    • Onion, finely chopped
    • Tomatoes, finely chopped
    • Ginger-garlic paste
    • Green chilies, slit
    • Chole masala
    • Turmeric powder
    • Red chili powder
    • Coriander powder
    • Amchur (dry mango powder)
    • Garam masala
    • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
    • Salt to taste
  • For Bhature:
    • All-purpose flour (maida)
    • Semolina (sooji)
    • Yogurt
    • Sugar
    • Baking powder
    • Salt
    • Oil for frying
  • For Serving:
    • Sliced onions
    • Green chilies
    • Lemon wedges

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Chole: Rinse and drain the soaked chickpeas. In a pressure cooker, add the chickpeas, water, salt, and turmeric powder. Pressure cook until chickpeas are tender.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Cook until fragrant.
  3. Add Spices: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy. Add chole masala, red chili powder, coriander powder, amchur, and garam masala. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Add Cooked Chickpeas: Add the cooked chickpeas along with some water. Simmer until the gravy thickens and flavors meld together.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Chole is ready to serve with bhature.
  6. Prepare the Bhature Dough: In a large bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, yogurt, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Knead into a soft dough using water. Let it rest for 2 hours.
  7. Fry the Bhature: Divide the dough into small balls. Roll out each ball into a circle. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the bhature until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  8. Serve: Serve hot chole with freshly fried bhature, sliced onions, green chilies, and lemon wedges. Enjoy the delicious and hearty meal!
